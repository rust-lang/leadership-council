### "Start a t-project-structure/t-comprehensibility" leadership-council#298

- **Link:** https://github.com/rust-lang/leadership-council/issues/298
- **Labels:** T-leadership-council, A-teams, A-project-structure, I-council-nominated

TC: We have David Wood with us. David, how can we help you?

David: The Council can decide whether I can do this.

TC: Thanks for your answers on the issue. For my part, I have one question before proposing FCP: Eric, is this a team you'd be interested in being a part of?

Eric: Have to think about it.

TC: I have a comment typed up to propose FCP merge. But it feels better to me to start a team with more people. Given the work you've done on this, perhaps we could add you tentatively — you could always drop off.

Eric: I can be tentatively added, but my time is limited. Each time I say "yes" to things, something else ends up becoming worse.

David: Like I said in the issue, anyone's welcome to contributed, and I can add them to the team.

oli: If the team starts with just David, people can be added later. David wants to get started, and that's good.

Josh: If this was a major area of responsibility that we need desperately to not be dropped on the floor, the maybe we should have more people on the team. When we are delegating a team to do something that we have failed to do for many years, and someone is graciously volunteering to do, so then if David suddenly has life circumstances change and can't do it, then it just goes back to being dropped on the floor, like it has for a long time. We are not delegating some great authority here. I don't think it is urgent or essential to push people onto this team beyond their availability and capacity. I think it would be appropriate for us to delegate to the team and then determine later on if it needs to expand or similar, especially if that becomes more critical than it currently is.

Lori: I'm happy to join David if this is a blocker. I've been focused on team health anyway.

Jack: Do we need a team right now? I'm curious if a team structure would help things, or if you just want the "yes, good idea"? What would a charter for the team look like? Thinking of Niko's Project Goals ask, and he said there's no team yet, can we do this as an experiment. And the team was chartered only after we realized that this is going to keep happening.

David: Team would help with structure. Discussions with compiler showed that structure would be helpful. It makes it easier for people to get involved. There's a detailed charter on the issue about what is expected.

Eric: This is not a blocker, but it's an interesting characterization that this team won't have responsibilities in terms of delegated authority. The way I see it, it's almost identical to the initiative that was done years ago where well-meaning people trying to write down the charters of teams in a consistent way faced pushback. That ended up burning people out. I trust David very much, and I don't think he'll do this, but the way it's written is identical to the previous attempt.

David: That's fair. I don't think we can prevent this structurally, just hope that people on the team will do a good job.

Josh: 1) I don't think there's a fundamental way to prevent this either; there's a similarity to the past attempt. The difference is that there's an explicit statement of what we're trying to do and what we're not (e.g. they're trying to help teams and *encourage* but not force alignment); that makes a difference. 2) The trust level in the Council and David is different than the trust level in the prior core team. 3) Want to ask, if an FCP is started on this, does anyone have blocking concerns on the number of people in the team? If not, I'd like to start an FCP.

Lori: When we had the All Hands and had the Teams session, there was a lot of positive feedback. People had "aha!" moments learning about the other teams. David's effort should improve the inter-team communication, and I think it's a very positive thing.

TC: That's good enough for me. I have the same reservations as Eric, and yet I'm happy to see how this works out and what David can do. I've proposed FCP merge.
