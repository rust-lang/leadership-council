### "Grants team and 2026 grants program" rfcs#3919

- **Link:** https://github.com/rust-lang/rfcs/pull/3919
- **Labels:** T-leadership-council, I-council-nominated

TC: Jakub has been doing the right thing and pinging me on this. I started revising to get this over the line, which sparked a couple of questions. I've been convinced that we, as the LC, should appoint members directly. That leaves the question of whether we want a separate Grants team and a team for handling the RFMF. Pros: there are two budgets — the RFMF money is coming from the outside (which means the team has to manage outward to some degree for those), and Grants (which are funds allocated by the council and so the team is accountable to the council for those). This could be a lot of work for one team. On the other hand, maybe the same people will be interested, and there's overhead in us choosing more people. I'm interested in hearing thoughts. This ties in with the fact that the RFMF RFC created the fund but didn't charter the team. If it's to be one team, I can handle that and charter the team in the same RFC. Otherwise, I'll keep the Grants team charter drafted separately.

Josh: I agree with the stated pros/cons. It seems like there's different, not fully overlapping work. If the same people are interested, it's fine, but having two teams gives people an option to be on one or both.

Niko: I've come to the opinion that we should have a funding team that thinks strategically and owns the overall process; while the selection process is a matter of a jury system.

TC: Last time we talked about this, you and I said we'd talk between us about this, so we could align drafting, but unfortunately that never happened. Do you think we need to do that?

Niko: I don't know, want to hear Lori's thoughts. Don't want to overthink.

Lori: IMO, I like the idea of one team. The Foundation would have to sit down with however many teams you create. You have three different things, and having one body that can see everything and prioritize would be better for funds distribution than if the two teams worked in silos. I observe that the Project doesn't talk to itself that much if you're on different teams.

Josh: I want to check something Niko said. Are you suggesting that we hand the combined Grants/RFMF budget to the Funding team to be able to make decisions, or are you suggesting two separate budgets managed by both?

Niko: I meant to give it the whole thing.

Josh: There's a compelling case for a unified team. The only hesitation is that I don't want to redesign the teams without putting something together first.

oli: It's already the plan we agreed on to handle two budgets together (in the RFC). I think we should make the election process part of the team, and make it more complex later. We need to make sure that long term this doesn't cause issues.

TC: My reading was that we were putting the RFMF funds under the Council's discretion with an expectation we'd be spending them on maintainer activities. While the RFMF budget comes from the outside, the Grants budget is something the LC needs to allocate from the Project Priorities budget. Niko, would your proposal be that the LC delegates some budget to the Funding team every year, and that team decides how to allocate that between grants and maintainers?

Niko: Pretty much. LC gets undirected funds, RFMF adds directed funds, you can choose to direct some undirected funds towards that. That money should go to the Funding team, which chooses how to allocate it. Let's make the team and let them own the process. The LC delegates to them, and they can move quickly to raise money.

Jack: I'd also say that I don't think the LC needs to say ahead of time how much they'll put towards the Grants team. I think it's less rigid than what TC said. The Council should be aware of the upper/lower limit of what they want to allocate.

Josh: +1 to create the team first, then have them propose a plan which we approve.

TC: I like the general idea. I don't want to do exactly that. We should be screening people for a team based on what we're chartering that team to do. We could appoint people to go draft a charter, then we could approve that charter.

Lori: Can I ask that we put a deadline to this? It's May 8th, we have money, a bunch of maintainers who could use the money. If we don't put a timeline on this, we could lose the people that might use the money.

Jack: I disagree. If an RFC to create a Grants program is something they think is a good way to fund maintainers, then it's fine to delegate. I don't think it makes sense to have them come up with a charter and then do work. I think we have a good idea of what kinds of things the team might be doing. Let's start getting stuff done.
